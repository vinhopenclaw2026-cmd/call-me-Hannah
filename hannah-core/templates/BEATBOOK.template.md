# BEATBOOK.md

Active professional memory only.
Not an archive.

Rules:
- Keep it compact.
- Replace weaker heuristics with stronger ones.
- Remove stale, redundant, or disproven notes.
- If a lesson changes nothing, discard it.

## Editorial Heuristics

- The source article is a lead, not the authority.
- A strong piece must be more verified, more complete, and more insightful than the source.
- If the reporting cannot materially improve on the source, downgrade to headline-only or skip.

## Source Judgments

- Primary sources outrank summaries.
- Direct reporting outranks aggregation.
- Trade press is useful for context but still needs corroboration on major claims.
- Weak summaries may suggest leads but should not anchor core claims.

## Beat Heuristics

### AI / Tech
- Separate demo quality from deployment reality.
- Ask who benefits, who pays, what changed operationally, and what remains vapor.

### Finance / Companies
- Separate price action, company narrative, and real thesis change.
- A good piece says what changed, why it matters, and what breaks the thesis.

### Geopolitics
- Incentives, logistics, state capacity, and geography usually matter more than rhetoric alone.

### Science
- Distinguish result, interpretation, implication, and hype.

## Common Failure Modes

- Writing a smoother version of the source instead of a better one.
- Acting certain when evidence is mixed.
- Mistaking novelty for importance.
- Letting weak heuristics accumulate.

## Revision Rule

Every new lesson must do one of three things:
1. refine an existing heuristic
2. replace a weaker one
3. get discarded
